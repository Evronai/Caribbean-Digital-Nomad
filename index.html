Chapter 1

What is a digital nomad? — definition & 2026 context

A digital nomad is a professional who works remotely using digital technologies while maintaining a lifestyle of travel and changing locations [1]. The term was coined in 1997 by Tsugio Makimoto and David Manners, who predicted "thousands would sell their homes and embrace a lifestyle of global mobility" [1].

In 2026, remote work is a permanent pillar of the global economy. According to the Jamaica Observer, remote job boards are gateways to international labour markets, allowing Caribbean professionals to earn foreign income without emigrating [2].

Global numbers: 35 million digital nomads in 2022; projected to exceed 1 billion by 2035 [1]. 58% are under 35.

Remote work has become mainstream — the bedroom can be anywhere, even Bali [1].

The 2026 reality: fractional work is mainstream. Caribbean professionals now work 2–4 part-time remote roles, earning $500–$3,000 per client, 10–25 hours per week [7]. AI acts as a "pocket coworker" enabling this juggle [7].

Chapter 5

How to choose your destination

✅ Factors to consider

  • Time zone alignment with clients [7]
  • Internet reliability – 100+ Mbps [9]
  • Cost of living vs. income [9]
  • Safety & political stability [2][6]
  • Tax implications (foreign income exemption) [2]
  • Healthcare quality

📋 Practical steps

  • Verify on official embassy sites [9]
  • Prepare police clearance, bank statements, insurance
  • Use Numbeo for real‑time cost comparisons
