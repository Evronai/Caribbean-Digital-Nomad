Chapter 1

What is a digital nomad? — definition & 2026 context

A digital nomad is a professional who works remotely using digital technologies while maintaining a lifestyle of travel and changing locations [1]. The term was coined in 1997 by Tsugio Makimoto and David Manners, who predicted "thousands would sell their homes and embrace a lifestyle of global mobility" [1].

In 2026, remote work is a permanent pillar of the global economy. According to the Jamaica Observer, remote job boards are gateways to international labour markets, allowing Caribbean professionals to earn foreign income without emigrating [2].

Global numbers: 35 million digital nomads in 2022; projected to exceed 1 billion by 2035 [1]. 58% are under 35.

Remote work has become mainstream — the bedroom can be anywhere, even Bali [1].

The 2026 reality: fractional work is mainstream. Caribbean professionals now work 2–4 part-time remote roles, earning $500–$3,000 per client, 10–25 hours per week [7]. AI acts as a "pocket coworker" enabling this juggle [7].

Chapter 5

How to choose your destination

✅ Factors to consider

  • Time zone alignment with clients/employer [7]
  • Internet reliability – aim for 100+ Mbps [9]
  • Cost of living vs. income – Southeast Asia lowest, Europe premium [9]
  • Safety & political stability – check Global Peace Index [2][6]
  • Tax implications – some countries exempt foreign income [2]
  • Healthcare quality – private insurance often required

📋 Practical steps

  • Verify requirements on official embassy websites – rules change frequently [9]
  • Prepare documents: police clearance, bank statements, insurance, employment proof
  • Consider using immigration specialists for complex applications [1]
  • Use Numbeo and Expatistan for real‑time cost comparisons
  • Join local expat Facebook groups to gauge safety and community

Chapter 6

Skills required in 2026

The remote work landscape now demands a blend of technical, self‑management, and AI‑literacy skills.

Core competencies

  • Digital collaboration: Slack, Zoom, Asana, Notion — asynchronous communication [3].
  • Self‑discipline & time management: essential when no boss watches [1].
  • AI proficiency: 2026 employers expect candidates to delegate routine tasks to AI (Claude, ChatGPT) to focus on high‑value work [7].
  • Income diversification: fractional / multi‑client mindset [7].
  • Cross‑cultural communication: working with global teams.

Technical skills in demand

  • Software development (Python, JavaScript, cloud).
  • UX/UI design & branding [2].
  • Digital marketing, SEO, content strategy.
  • Data analysis & AI prompting.
  • Online teaching / curriculum design [6].
